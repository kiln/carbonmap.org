The
carbon map
Making sense of climate change responsibility and vulnerability
A Kiln Project

What's this site?

This website is designed to help convey how different countries fit into the climate change picture – both the causes and the risks. We had the idea for it after becoming frustrated with he inadequacies of choropleths ('shaded-in maps') at conveying data in an engaging and clear way. The impetus for actually building it came from the World Bank's Apps for Climate competition.

Who made it?

The site was created by Duncan Clark and Robin Houston under the banner of Kiln, a project focusing on interesting new ways of presenting information.

Why do you have so many measures for climate change responsibility?

Climate change responsibility is conventionally discussed simply in terms of national emissions or emissions per capita. We feel that doesn't give the whole picture, because it ignores other factors such as historical emissions and the importing and exporting of fossil fuels. To give a fuller and more meaningful picture, this site helps you compare lots of different ways of thinking about responsibility for emissions.

Has this kind of mapping been done before?

The site is based on cartograms: maps distorted to reflect a dataset. There's nothing new about cartograms but to our knowledge this is the first website to display animated, interactive versions.

How does it work?

We create cartograms from datasets using an algorithm by Mark Newman (which works, ingeniously, by simulating the diffusion of chemicals between areas, as described in this paper by Newman and Michael Gastner). Then we convert those to interactive SVG maps and apply animations, shading, clickability, etc.

Can I make my own version?

Not now but maybe one day. We're exploring the possibility of creating a tool to generate these types of maps automatically from a spreadsheet.
